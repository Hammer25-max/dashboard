SUPPLEMENT SHOPPING GUIDE

50-Year-Old Athlete · Anti-Inflammatory Protocol · T2 Diabetes (Januvia 100mg)

💉 Januvia Safety Check: All supplements listed below are considered safe with Januvia 100mg (sitagliptin). However, always confirm new supplements with your doctor, especially if your HbA1c or blood sugar management changes. Do NOT add Berberine without physician approval — it can over-potentiate blood sugar lowering when combined with medication.

MAGNESIUM GLYCINATE

Improves deep sleep, reduces muscle cramps, supports cardiovascular health. Glycinate form is best absorbed and gentlest on the stomach.
Magnesium deficiency is directly linked to insulin resistance. Supplementing magnesium measurably improves blood sugar control in T2D. This is one of the most important supplements for you.

10–15g per day · always paired with 500mg Vitamin C

Vitamin C taken alongside increases collagen synthesis 3–4× (Stanford research). Best injury prevention tool available. No flavor, dissolves in any liquid.
⏰ Add to morning shake 30–60 min before exercise for maximum joint benefit

ZINC + COPPER (PAIRED)
Zinc: 15–25mg + Copper: 1–2mg — always take together
Zinc is essential for insulin production and glucose metabolism — frequently depleted in T2D patients. Runners lose zinc in sweat. Low zinc = impaired insulin signaling. Copper MUST be taken alongside zinc to maintain mineral balance — never supplement zinc alone long-term. Also supports testosterone at 50, immune function, and wound healing.
Zinc is directly involved in insulin production. T2D patients are frequently zinc-deficient. Addressing this zinc gap may improve insulin signaling alongside Januvia.
