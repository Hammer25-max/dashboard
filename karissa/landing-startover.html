The Psychology Behind Why It Keeps Happening

The real reason
it hasn't stuck yet.

This isn't a character flaw. It's a science problem. Here's what's actually going on.

The Core Mechanism
Self-Efficacy
Psychologist Albert Bandura identified that every time you keep a promise to yourself, your brain collects evidence that you are someone who can be trusted. When you stop, that evidence goes the other way. The goal is building the evidence stack -- not perfection.
Why Identity Beats Motivation
Identity Shift
James Clear showed that lasting change happens at the identity level. You stop trying to be someone who works out and start being someone who works out. That shift is what makes skipping feel wrong instead of normal. The system builds that identity intentionally.
What The Prefrontal Cortex Does
Gets Stronger
Every time you choose the harder thing -- showing up when you don't feel like it -- you're literally strengthening the part of your brain that controls follow-through. This is why discipline gets easier over time. Not because the hard things get easier. Because you get better at doing hard things.
The Research-Backed Phenomenon
Keystone Habits
Charles Duhigg's research on keystone habits showed that one consistent behavior creates ripple effects across every other area of life. The gym is not the final goal. It's the door that opens everything else -- eating, sleep, focus, confidence, work, relationships.
Why Small Actions Produce Big Results

Discipline compounds
like interest.
